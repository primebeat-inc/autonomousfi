# Glossary

**agent**: An autonomous software entity that holds state and executes actions on behalf of a principal.
**escrow**: A locked balance held by a neutral contract that releases funds only when defined conditions are met.
**hostage stake**: Collateral posted by an agent that can be forfeited if it misbehaves, raising the cost of cheap identities.
**hybrid mechanism**: A protocol combining personhood proofs with hostage collateral to recover honest behavior under cheap identity.
**paidAgent**: An agent that has posted a hostage stake and is therefore eligible to earn rewards under the hybrid mechanism.
**Plasma**: A scaling design where child chains commit periodic state roots to a parent chain and users exit via fraud proofs.
**PoP (Proof of Personhood)**: A cryptographic attestation that a participant is a unique human, used to limit sybil multiplication.
**QVAC**: Quadratic Voting with Anti-Collusion, a governance primitive that pairs quadratic voting weights with collusion-resistant constraints.
**reputation**: A score summarizing past behavior used to weight future trust, rewards, or access.
**settle**: Finalize the outcome of an interaction, releasing escrow and updating balances and reputation.
**slash**: Forfeit part or all of a stake as a penalty for provable misbehavior.
**sybil**: A single actor controlling many identities to inflate influence, votes, or rewards.
**Whuffie problem**: The impossibility of sustaining honest reputation economies when identities are cheap and unbounded.
**Worldcoin**: A personhood system that uses iris-scan biometrics to issue a unique human credential.
**zkPassport**: A personhood proof derived from a government passport using zero-knowledge proofs to preserve privacy.
